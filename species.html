Six trees of
Cyprus

A reference guide to the tree species detected by Arbor — their ecology, habitat, cultural history, and role in the Cypriot landscape.

🌳
Bayan
Ficus benghalensis
Ornamental Shade tree Introduced
A majestic fig known for its sweeping aerial roots, widely planted in Cypriot parks and public spaces for shade and grandeur.

🌿
Eucalyptus
Eucalyptus camaldulensis
Fast-growing Introduced Riparian
Introduced to Cyprus in the 19th century for drainage and timber, now one of the most recognisable trees across the island's lowlands.
View species
🌲
Pine
Pinus brutia
Native Dominant forest Honey production
The Calabrian pine dominates Cyprus's mountain forests, covering both the Troodos and Kyrenia ranges from sea level to 1,400m.
View species
🫒
Olive
Olea europaea
Native Cultivated Cultural symbol
Cultivated on Cyprus for over 2,000 years, the olive tree is deeply woven into the island's identity, cuisine, and folklore.
View species
🌴
Palm
Phoenix theophrasti
Endemic Coastal Protected
The Cretan date palm, endemic to the eastern Mediterranean, has one of its last remaining native stands at Lara Bay in the Akamas peninsula.
View species
🌲
Cypress
Cupressus sempervirens
Native Pentadaktylos Ancient symbol
The Mediterranean cypress is so tied to Cyprus that the island's very name may derive from it. Its slender silhouette defines the Kyrenia mountain skyline.
